Information about the three areas

Loneliness

Loneliness doesn’t always mean being alone: it’s the feeling of lacking meaningful companionship. Among older adults, it can arise due to loss of loved ones, reduced mobility, or retirement.

Suggestions: join local groups, schedule regular calls with family, participate in volunteer activities.

Depression

Depression includes persistent sadness, loss of interest, changes in appetite and sleep, and lack of energy. In older age, it can sometimes be mistaken for normal aging.

When to seek help: if symptoms last for weeks or affect daily life. Professionals can offer tailored therapy and, when needed, safe medication.

Anxiety

Anxiety can appear as restlessness, frequent worries, muscle tension, or trouble sleeping. Among older adults, concerns about health and independence are common.

Helpful techniques: slow breathing exercises, daily routines, social contact, and professional support when necessary.

If you suspect that you or a loved one is experiencing a serious issue, consult a doctor or mental health professional. This page is a visual model and does not replace professional care.
